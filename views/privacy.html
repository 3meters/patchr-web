Privacy Policy

Welcome to Teeny Social, a service provided by 3meters. Teeny Social is the social sharing app designed for people who want just enough sharing. We provide the Teeny Social Service (the "Service") to you under the Terms of Service . The Service is not available to children younger than 13.

Our Privacy Policy explains:

  • What information we collect.
  • How we use and share that information.
  • How you can access and update that information.

What Information Do We Collect?

  • Private Personal Information You Provide to Us: You may choose to provide us with private personal information, including your email address, phone number, and credit card information.
  • Content That You Add to the Service: You may choose to add content to the Service such as a public profile, messages, photos, videos and the like. We call this information Your Content.
  • Information We Get From Your Use of the Service: When you use the Service, Teeny Social automatically receives and may record information from your browser or mobile device, including your location, IP address, cookie information, and requests you make of the service.
  • Information We Receive from Third Parties: We receive and store information from third parties that interact in some way with the Service. For example, you may choose to launch another app from Teeny Social, and the owner of that app may share with us whether you did or did not successfully sign in to their service. Some apps may share your Personal Information, activities and/or content with Teeny Social. Please read the privacy policy of any such app so that you understand its sharing practices.

How do we use and share that information?

We use the information we collect to provide, maintain and improve the Service, to develop new services, and to protect Teeny Social and our users.

We will never sell or rent your Private Personal Information to anyone. We will only share your Private Personal Information if necessary to comply with laws, to enforce the Terms of Service if we believe in good faith that they have been violated, or to prevent fraud or security attacks.
